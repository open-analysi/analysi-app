# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

The Analysi project is committed to providing a welcoming, harassment-free
experience for everyone who participates in our community, regardless of
background or identity.

## Our Standards

We adopt the [Contributor Covenant, version 2.1][cc] as our community
standard. Please read it in full — it describes the behavior we expect from
contributors and the behavior we will not tolerate.

[cc]: https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/

In short: be respectful, assume good faith, give and accept constructive
feedback gracefully, and focus on what is best for the community.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces — the GitHub repository,
issue tracker, pull requests, discussions, and any other forum maintained by
the project — and also when an individual is officially representing the
project in public spaces.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct,
please report it privately to the maintainers at **openanalysi.security@gmail.com**.
Reports are handled confidentially. Include:

- What happened, where, and when.
- Who was involved (usernames or links are fine).
- Any supporting context (links, screenshots).

You will receive an acknowledgment within **5 business days**.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards.
They may take any action they deem appropriate, including warning the
offender, removing comments or commits, or banning a contributor temporarily
or permanently from project spaces.

Enforcement decisions follow the [Contributor Covenant Enforcement
Guidelines][guidelines].

[guidelines]: https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/#enforcement-guidelines
